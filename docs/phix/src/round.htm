round

Syntax: atom x2 = round(atom x1, atom inverted_precision=1)
Description: round x1 to the specified precision.
Comments: The precision is the inverse of what you might have expected, eg to round a number to the nearest 0.1, specify an inverted precision of 10, to round to the nearest 1000, specify 0.001.

This function may be applied to an atom or sq_round() to all elements of a sequence. The rules for sequence operations apply.
Example: 
r = round(1.4)          -- r is 1
r = round(1.6)          -- r is 2
r = round(8.136,100)    -- r is 8.14
Technicalia The precision is specified in inverted form for a simple practical reason: atoms cannot hold the exact value of a decimal fraction such as 0.01, which is particularly significant when attempting to round values near to the accuracy of the physical hardware, where a small deviation in the input can easily manifest as a large discrepancy in the result. While much the same effect can be observed both when rounding to the nearest very small amount, and when rounding to the nearest very large amount, the latter is much less likely in practice to be subsequently multiplied by a very big number, and is therefore deemed less critical.
